Pre-Implementation: Why the Victron Energy MultiPlus-II Inverter/Charger Powers Our Off-Grid Cabin

by
Nicholas F. Palomba
on February 22, 2026


There are many inverters on the market, but only one stood out as the best choice to meet our needs. After too many hours of research, Victron Energy's MultiPlus-II emerged as the optimal solution for my father's off-grid cabin. In fact, two have already been purchased and mounted in the electrical room.

An inverter is essential for converting available DC power, such as batteries, solar panels, and wind turbines, into the 120/240 V AC split-phase power typically used in residences. Key considerations included the following:

  • Price

  • Pure, grid-forming sine wave production for 120/240 V AC split-phase

  • Power rating

  • Power conversion efficiency

  • Peak power capability

  • Open communications compatibility for monitoring and control

  • High programmability

  • Manufacturer reputation

  • UL listing for electrical inspection purposes

As an electrical engineer, the key factor for me is Victron Energy's modular and semi-open platform, which enables the design of a DC system incorporating the optimal components that precisely deliver the desired features. For instance, the 48 V DC bus is external to the inverter, as are the solar charge controllers and battery. Fundamentally, this approach keeps our options virtually unlimited, as we selected an inverter that can be integrated into a modular design rather than be limited by the constrained features provided by an all-in-one unit. For example, a wind energy source can easily be coupled to the DC bus in the future. Additional advantages include the power flow bi-directionality (the integral charger and AC input compatible with typical portable gas generators), as well as the associated uninterruptible power supply (UPS) and PowerAssist functions.

Two MultiPlus-II 48/5000/70-95 120V inverter/chargers are needed working in tandem to produce 120/240 V AC split-phase power.
